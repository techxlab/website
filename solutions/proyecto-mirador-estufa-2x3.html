Solution Overview & Benefits

Dos por Tres cookstoves replace inefficient, smoky traditional Honduran mud stoves and according to third-party analysis, provide 10 times the stove cost in benefits to our 450,000 users. The benefits include reductions in respiratory illness, forest degradation (14-29 trees per stove), and carbon emissions (~13 mtCO2); as well as poverty alleviation through reduced fuelwood consumption and the creation of 17 microenterprises and 130 jobs. Proyecto Mirador, a non-profit organization, sells Gold Standard carbon offsets to construct in-situ Dos por Tres plancha, chimney type stoves in poor, rural Honduras. Mirador builds over 2,000 stoves a month and has two years of backlog in stove requests. Families co-invest in stove construction with materials and time; no cash changes hands with our beneficiaries. Mirador emphasizes training and maintenance to ensure stoves survive for 5 years or more.

History & Development

Proyecto Mirador is a grassroots organization that grew organically after the founders were in Honduras as translators for medical clinics and saw a disproportionate number of cases of respiratory illness, particularly among women and children, in the clinic. Their daughter, Skye, was invited into a home and experienced first hand the smoke filled rooms, causing her to recognize the source of so many illnesses. Thus, Mirador was conceived and began to install the La Justa rocket stove, which was later adapted to improve efficiency and durability. Mirador's proprietary adaptation of the original La Justa is called the Dos por Tres. It is a plancha style stove, built in situ, that is functionally the same as the traditional, inefficient stove that is replaced and thus, easily assimilated and positively received by the beneficiary.
